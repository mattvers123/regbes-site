The REGBES approach
Flexible shore power coverage for mixed fleets — without fixed infrastructure

Most ports serve more than one vessel type. Rather than installing fixed OPS infrastructure at every berth, REGBES mobile CMS units can be deployed flexibly across your terminal — each configured for a specific vessel type, IEC standard, and plug type. Add units as your traffic grows. Redeploy them as your fleet mix changes.

No fixed infrastructure per berth
Serve multiple vessel types from one terminal
Configure each unit per IEC standard and plug type
Scale your fleet as port traffic grows
Product variants
Four variants. Every application covered.

Each REGBES CMS variant is engineered for a specific deployment context — from narrow container terminal aisles to configurable port and barge deployments, heavy-duty cable reel installations, and elevated delivery by mobile crane arm.

Variant 01
Container terminals · Narrow berths
Compact Mobile CMS

Dual-reel motorised system on a compact mobile platform, designed for narrow-aisle container terminal berths where space between vessel and quay is constrained. Two cables are deployed simultaneously, reducing connection time and supporting high vessel turnaround. The mobile platform requires no fixed quayside installation — it can be repositioned across the terminal as needed.

Narrow profile — fits tight terminal aisles
Dual-reel — two cables deployed simultaneously
Motorised with integrated control panel
IEC 80005-1 compliant
Mobile platform — no fixed installation required
✓ Delivered to MARDAS Container Terminal, Istanbul

Variant 02
Ports · Barges · Offshore · Configurable
Modular Mobile CMS

A configurable reel unit with interchangeable cable delivery methods — crane arm, roller system, or custom configuration depending on berth layout and vessel type. Width is not constrained, making it deployable at quayside berths, container terminals, and barge decks without modification. Cable count and plug type are configured per target vessel and IEC standard, making it suitable for ports that serve a mixed fleet.

Interchangeable cable delivery method
No width constraint — flexible deployment at ports and barges
Configurable cable count per IEC standard
IEC 80005-1 and IEC 80005-3 variants available

Variant 03
Port-to-ship · Ship-to-port · Heavy duty
Cable Reel

Heavy-duty motorised cable reel for port-to-ship and ship-to-port power transfer. Shore power cables carry high currents and are heavy — safe and controlled deployment, tensioning, and retrieval requires motorised handling. The REGBES cable reel manages this reliably, as a standalone product or as part of a larger OPS installation. Compatible with both fixed quayside installations and mobile CMS configurations.

Motorised — handles heavy-duty industrial shore power cables
Port-to-ship and ship-to-port configurations
Integrated motorised control panel
IEC 80005-1 and IEC 80005-3 compatible
Standalone or integrated with mobile CMS platform

Variant 04
Elevated delivery · High freeboard · Third-party crane integration · Cruise · Ro-Pax
Crane Arm CMS

For vessels with high freeboard where quayside-level cable deployment is not practical, REGBES integrates a third-party mobile crane with its cable management system to deliver the shore power cable at the correct height. The crane type — reach, lifting capacity, and mounting configuration — is selected based on vessel height, berth geometry, and operational requirements. REGBES handles the cable management and control integration; the crane is sourced and configured per project. Used in combination with REGBES cable reels for complete cable handling from reel to plug.

Third-party crane integration — type selected per application
Elevated cable delivery — reach and capacity matched to vessel and berth
Used in combination with REGBES cable reels
Mobile and repositionable — no fixed quayside installation required
Cruise ships, Ro-Pax, and large offshore vessel applications
